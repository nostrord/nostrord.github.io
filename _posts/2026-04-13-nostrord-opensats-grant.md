Nostrord started with a simple idea. Group chat on Nostr should feel as good as the centralized apps people are leaving behind, without asking anyone to give up ownership of their communities.

Today that idea got a real push. [OpenSats](https://opensats.org/blog/sixteenth-wave-of-nostr-grants){:target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"} selected Nostrord for their sixteenth wave of Nostr grants. Thank you. This gives the project room to grow from a working beta into something the Nostr community can actually rely on.

## What is Nostrord

If you're just finding us: Nostrord is a Kotlin Multiplatform client for NIP-29 relay-based groups. A Discord-style experience on Nostr, but decentralized and built on a protocol you actually own.

Chat, replies, moderation, relay-enforced membership and permissions all work today. A public beta is live at [web.nostrord.com](https://web.nostrord.com/){:target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"}, and we're building across platforms.

## What the grant enables

Room to do things right. That means maturing the Kotlin Multiplatform architecture, filling out the NIP-29 feature set, tightening performance across platforms, and listening to what the community is asking for as we head toward a stable release.
